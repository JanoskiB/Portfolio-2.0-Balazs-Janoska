Barbless Gear Webshop

Concept made with Figma and Adobe Illustrator

A problem to solve

It has been my life long dream to have my own fishing brand. A webshop that sells perfectly crafted hand-made lures, mostly from recycled materials, as well as ethically made premium quality clothing for anglers. My idea was well recieved by some of the best craftsmen from Hungary and Japan, so it was time for me to start working on the UI and identity of the webshop.

Development process

Before making the wireframe of the webshop, I analyzed the best fishing stores on the web. The bench-marking let me understand what are the common traits of a visually pleasing online store, such as a plain white background that works well with the featured images that also always have white background. After the initial ideation process, I made the wireframe of the most important pages with Figma. I used a grid system for the interface and filled some parts with content to help visualize the final version.

After having most of the website’s wireframes, it was finally time to decide on the visual identity of the brand. I used Adobe Stock to get licensed artworks, and then edited them with Adobe Illustrator to create a logo and other icons that fit well with the identity of the brand. As for the color palette of the webshop, I used an online tool called Coolors to get a palette that harmonizes with nature and purity, which are main themes of the brand.


Next, I started making the high fidelity concept of the Barbless Gear webshop's landing page. I paid close attention to keeping the visual identity clear and minimalistic to make the brand feel professional and to make it easy for the users to find everything. For the featured images I used content from Unsplash and images from lure-makers that I will co-operate with. I also used a Figma plugin called Feather Icons that offers an open source library of professionally designed vector icons.
